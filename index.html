About Us
Designed around the rider, not just the helmet.

An intelligent helmet that responds when the rider cannot.

Motorcycle delivery riders in Qatar operate in demanding conditions. In a crash or medical emergency, response time is critical — especially if the rider cannot reach their phone.

RAKIB integrates embedded sensors, on-device intelligence, GPS, and cellular connectivity into a single safety system. It continuously monitors motion and vital signs, detects abnormal conditions, and automatically triggers an SOS alert with the rider's location.

Problem

Emergency response can be delayed.

A rider may be unable to access their phone or call for help in time.

Approach

Move safety decisions directly into the helmet.

The system performs real-time monitoring and detection independently of the rider.

Outcome

Faster, reliable emergency response.

RAKIB detects danger and initiates alerts automatically, reducing response time and reliance on manual action.

Project Timeline

From concept to working prototype.

Two semesters. Seventeen phases. One fully working AI helmet.

ECEN 403 — Fall 2025
Phase 1 — Research & Initial PlanningAug 21 – Sep 1

Formed the team, confirmed mentorship, and conducted a literature review to establish the project concept.

Phase 2 — System Concept & Component SelectionSep 1 – Sep 30

Finalized the system architecture, selected hardware components, and placed orders.

Phase 3 — Midterm Prep & Component ArrivalOct 1 – Oct 17

Delivered the midterm presentation, received components, and began initial sensor testing.

Phase 4 — Hardware IntegrationOct 17 – Nov 1

Integrated all sensors, established wireless connectivity, and calibrated the motion detection hardware.

Phase 5 — AI Model DevelopmentNov 1 – Nov 17

Collected sensor data, defined medical safety thresholds, and trained AI models for crash and vitals detection.

Phase 6 — System IntegrationNov 17 – Nov 22

Built the mobile app, deployed AI models to the cloud, and completed the full helmet-to-app data pipeline.

Phase 7 — Finalization & TestingNov 22 – Dec 7

Completed end-to-end testing, validated battery life, tuned detection thresholds, and finalized the helmet assembly.

ECEN 404 — Spring 2026
Phase 8 — Open-Source Dataset CollectionJan 4 – Jan 31

Collected and reviewed publicly available datasets to support model training and validation.

Phase 9 — Temperature Sensor Testing & 3D PrintingJan 31 – Feb 15

Validated temperature sensor accuracy and designed 3D-printed enclosures for the helmet internals.

Phase 10 — Threshold Definition & Detection TestingJan 31 – Feb 28

Defined medical thresholds for vital signs based on clinical guidelines and validated detection on hardware.

Phase 11 — Motion Testing (Walking & Head Motion)Feb 15 – Feb 28

Conducted real-world motion tests with the helmet worn to distinguish normal activity from crash signatures.

Phase 12 — AI Model Design & TrainingFeb 15 – Mar 15

Designed the machine learning pipeline, trained and validated AI models, and deployed to the cloud.

Phase 13 — App Development & BLE IntegrationFeb 28 – Mar 15

Developed the full iOS app with live vitals, crash alerts, ride history, SOS scenarios, and an AI voice assistant.

Phase 14 — Helmet-App Communication IntegrationMar 15 – Mar 31

Fully integrated helmet firmware with the iOS app, verifying sensor streaming, event handling, and SOS state transitions.

Phase 15 — Hardware-Software Final IntegrationMar 15 – Apr 15

Assembled all hardware inside the helmet shell, resolved integration issues, and verified full system operation.

Phase 16 — System Validation & Accuracy TestingMar 15 – Apr 15

Validated the full system across crash detection, vitals monitoring, and SOS delivery scenarios.

Phase 17 — Final Demo & Design ExpoApr 2026

Demonstrated the working prototype at the ECEN 404 Design Expo.
